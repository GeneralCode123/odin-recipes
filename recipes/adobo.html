Pork Adobo

Pork Adobo is a classic Filipino dish known for its rich, savory flavor and tender meat. It is traditionally made by simmering chunks of pork—often a combination of fatty and lean cuts—in a marinade of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. As it cooks, the meat absorbs the bold, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors of the sauce, resulting in a deeply flavorful dish with a glossy, caramelized finish.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns (or ground black pepper, to taste)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (optional, for sautéing)
  • 1 tsp brown sugar (optional, for a hint of sweetness)
  • Salt, to taste (adjust depending on the saltiness of the soy sauce)

Steps

  1. Marinate the pork with soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and pepper for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pot and sauté garlic until golden (optional for added flavor).
  3. Add the marinated pork with the marinade and cook until the meat is lightly browned.
  4. Pour in water, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer until the pork becomes tender.
  5. Uncover, adjust seasoning, and let the sauce reduce until thick and flavorful.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your delicious pork adobo.
