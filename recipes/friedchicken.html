Fried Chicken

Fried Chicken is a classic comfort food made by marinating chicken pieces in buttermilk for tenderness, then coating them in seasoned flour and deep-frying until the outside is crispy and golden brown. The result is juicy, flavorful meat wrapped in a crunchy, savory crust that’s loved worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into serving pieces) or 2 lbs chicken parts
  • 1 cup buttermilk or 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp vinegar (for marinating)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • Cooking oil (for deep frying)

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken in buttermilk (or milk with vinegar) for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  2. In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and pepper.
  3. Remove chicken from marinade and coat each piece evenly in the flour mixture.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Fry chicken pieces in batches until golden brown and cooked through (about 10–15 minutes).
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
